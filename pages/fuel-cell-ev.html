Fuel Cell Electric Vehicles (FCEVs)

Electric vehicles powered by hydrogen fuel cells that produce electricity with only water as an emission

Hydrogen-Powered Innovation

FCEVs use hydrogen fuel cells to generate electricity that powers an electric motor. These vehicles combine hydrogen stored in high-pressure tanks with oxygen from the air to produce electricity, with water vapor as the only emission. They offer quick refueling and long-range capabilities similar to conventional vehicles.

Key Specifications

Power Source: Hydrogen fuel cell + small battery
Range: 300-400 miles per fill
Refueling Time: About 5 minutes
Emissions: Water vapor only

Advantages

  • Zero harmful emissions (water is the only byproduct)
  • Quick refueling (similar to conventional vehicles)
  • Long driving range comparable to gas vehicles
  • Quiet operation like other electric vehicles
  • No battery degradation concerns
  • Potential for renewable hydrogen production

Challenges

  • Limited hydrogen refueling infrastructure
  • Higher cost compared to other EV types
  • Current hydrogen production often relies on fossil fuels
  • Less energy efficient than battery electric vehicles
  • Limited vehicle model availability

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs)

Self-charging hybrids that combine a gasoline engine with an electric motor to improve efficiency

Efficient Self-Charging Technology

HEVs combine a conventional gasoline engine with an electric motor that assists during acceleration and captures energy during braking. Unlike PHEVs, traditional hybrids cannot be plugged in to charge and have smaller batteries that are charged by the engine and regenerative braking.

Key Specifications

Power Source: Gasoline engine + small battery
Electric-Only Range: 1-3 miles (limited)
Total Range: 500-700 miles
Emissions: Lower than conventional vehicles

Advantages

  • No need for charging infrastructure
  • Better fuel economy than conventional vehicles
  • Lower emissions than conventional vehicles
  • Familiar driving experience
  • Self-charging through regenerative braking
  • Minimal lifestyle adjustments needed

Challenges

  • Limited electric-only driving capability
  • Still dependent on gasoline
  • Higher complexity than conventional vehicles
  • Slightly higher upfront cost than conventional vehicles
  • Not eligible for as many incentives as PHEVs or BEVs

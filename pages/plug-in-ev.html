Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs)

Versatile vehicles with both electric battery and gasoline engine that can be plugged in to charge

The Best of Both Worlds

PHEVs combine the benefits of electric and conventional vehicles, featuring both a battery-powered electric motor and a gasoline engine. They can be charged from an external power source and run on electricity for shorter trips while using gasoline for longer journeys.

Key Specifications

Power Source: Battery pack + gasoline engine
Electric Range: 20-60 miles
Total Range: 300-600+ miles
Emissions: Zero emissions in electric mode, low emissions in hybrid mode

Advantages

  • Eliminates range anxiety with gasoline backup
  • Electric-only driving for daily commutes
  • Lower emissions than conventional vehicles
  • Reduced fuel costs compared to gas-only vehicles
  • No need for frequent charging on long trips
  • Potential tax incentives

Challenges

  • Higher maintenance needs than BEVs
  • More complex systems than conventional vehicles
  • Lower electric range than BEVs
  • Higher upfront cost than conventional vehicles
  • Requires both charging and gas station infrastructure
